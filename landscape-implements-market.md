<p><p>The landscape implements market is primarily divided into front-tine and rear-tine categories, each serving distinct purposes for soil cultivation. Front-tine tillers feature tines positioned at the front, providing agility and ease of maneuvering, making them suitable for smaller gardens and tight spaces. In contrast, rear-tine tillers have tines located at the rear, offering greater power and stability for larger areas, effectively breaking up tough soil. Both types cater to varying user needs based on garden size and soil conditions.</p></p>
