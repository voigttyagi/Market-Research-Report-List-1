<p><p>The passive RFID tags market is segmented into inlays and hard tags. Inlays are flexible and typically comprise an RFID chip attached to a substrate, commonly used in labels or cards for applications like inventory tracking and access control. Hard tags, on the other hand, are robust and encased in durable materials, making them suitable for harsh environments and high-value assets. Both types leverage radio waves for communication, facilitating efficient data collection without a power source.</p></p>
