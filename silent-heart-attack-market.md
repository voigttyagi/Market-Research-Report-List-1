<p><p>Silent heart attacks, which occur without noticeable symptoms, can be identified through various diagnostic imaging methods. Electrocardiography (ECG) measures electrical activity to detect heart damage. Chest X-rays provide a visual assessment of heart size and structure. Computed Tomography (CT scans) offer detailed cross-sectional images of the heart. Echocardiography uses ultrasound to visualize heart function and blood flow. Other methods may include blood tests or advanced imaging techniques, facilitating early detection and management of cardiac conditions.</p></p>
