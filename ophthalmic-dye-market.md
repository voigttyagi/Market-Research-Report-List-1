<p><p>The ophthalmic dye market includes several types, each serving distinct purposes in eye care. Fluorescein dye is commonly used for detecting corneal injuries and assessing tear stability. Rose Bengal stain highlights damaged epithelial cells and helps diagnose dry eye conditions. Trypan blue stain is employed during cataract surgery to differentiate between live and dead cells. Other dyes in the market may include indocyanine green and sodium fluorescein, catering to various diagnostic and surgical applications in ophthalmology.</p></p>
