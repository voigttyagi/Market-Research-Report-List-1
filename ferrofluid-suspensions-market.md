<p><p>Ferrofluid suspensions can be categorized based on their surfactant types and ionic characteristics. Surfactant-type ferrofluids utilize stabilizing agents, which reduce particle agglomeration, improving performance in various applications like seals and sensors. Ionic-type ferrofluids leverage ionic interactions for stability, often manipulating charge to enhance dispersion. Both types serve crucial roles in fields such as electronics, biomedical applications, and engineering, with unique properties influencing their effectiveness and suitability for specific uses in technology and research.</p></p>
