<p><ul><li>Continuous Automation</li><li>Intermittent Automation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Machine and factory automation can be categorized into two primary market types: continuous automation and intermittent automation. Continuous automation involves processes that run non-stop, ideal for high-volume production environments, such as oil refineries or chemical plants, where consistent output is crucial. In contrast, intermittent automation is used for batch production, allowing for flexible manufacturing processes with varying production schedules. This approach suits industries requiring customization and lower volume outputs, such as automotive assembly or food processing.</p></p>
