<p><p>Factor VIII deficiency, commonly known as Hemophilia A, is a genetic disorder characterized by the inability of the blood to clot properly due to insufficient levels of clotting factor VIII. Treatment primarily involves replacing the missing factor through infusions of Factor VIII concentrates, which can be derived from human plasma or produced via recombinant technology. Advances in treatment have led to the development of longer-acting formulations and gene therapy, offering patients improved outcomes and reduced frequency of infusions.</p><p>The Factor VIII Deficiency Treatment Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.6% during the forecast period.
