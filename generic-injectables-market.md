<p><p>The generic injectables market is categorized into two main types: small molecules and large molecules. Small molecule injectables are typically low molecular weight drugs that are chemically synthesized and easily absorbed by the body. They usually treat a wide range of conditions. In contrast, large molecule injectables consist of biologics, such as proteins or antibodies, derived from living organisms and used for more complex diseases like cancer or autoimmune disorders. Both segments are essential for accessible healthcare.</p></p>
